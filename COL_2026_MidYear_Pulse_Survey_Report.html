Key Themes from Written Feedback

Employees shared candid, thoughtful feedback in open-ended responses. Below are the themes that surfaced across multiple departments — organized by what's working and where we can improve. Individual responses remain confidential.

Strength
📈
Positive Momentum
Many employees across departments noticed real improvements over the past 6 months — better organization, stronger communication from management, and a sense that the company is gaining traction. The growth with Crash and expanded inventory investment were specifically called out as wins.
Strength
🤝
Local Leadership Wins
Several teams shared that their direct managers and supervisors are doing a great job — patient, clear, and supportive. When employees feel respected and heard by their immediate manager, it shows up in their scores. Departments with strong local leadership consistently rated higher across the board.
Strength
🧭
Clarity of Role
Role clarity is our highest-scoring dimension company-wide (4.22/5). Most employees understand what's expected of them — a foundational element for performance and confidence. This is something we should continue to protect as roles evolve.
Opportunity
💻
Technology & Equipment
Outdated tablets, slow scanners, and unreliable software were the single most consistent theme across operational teams. Employees want tools that match the pace of work. Investing in reliable equipment is directly tied to safety, efficiency, and employee morale — this is a high-ROI fix.
Opportunity
📅
Regular Team Touchpoints
Employees across multiple departments asked for more structured communication — recurring team meetings, goal-setting sessions, and honest feedback on performance. Many feel decisions happen above them without context. Even brief, consistent check-ins can dramatically improve trust and engagement.
Opportunity
👥
Staffing & Cross-Training
Several departments — especially Shipping and Sales — flagged that being short-staffed creates a ripple effect: routes get overloaded, service slips, and stress builds. Cross-training drivers and warehouse staff to cover each other in a pinch was a practical solution that employees themselves proposed.
Needs Attention
⚖️
Accountability & Consistency
Accountability is our lowest company-wide score (3.41/5). Employees want to see rules and expectations applied consistently — across all roles and levels. When people feel the same standards don't apply to everyone, it erodes trust quickly. Clear, visible follow-through matters.
Needs Attention
💰
Compensation & Recognition
Compensation and benefits were raised across multiple departments. Employees who feel they've grown in their roles want to see that reflected in their pay and career path. Transparent raise criteria, recognition programs, and a clear path forward go a long way — especially for long-tenured, high-performing team members.
Needs Attention
🔗
Cross-Department Collaboration
Teams often feel siloed from one another. Employees in operations, sales, and back-office roles all noted that better integration between departments would improve both their day-to-day experience and the customer experience. More shared visibility, processes, and communication between teams is a recurring ask.
What We're Doing About It
Our Commitment
Surveys only matter if they lead to action. Below are the commitments leadership is making based on what we heard. We'll check in on progress at our next all-hands and in the end-of-year survey.
1
Equipment & Technology Audit
We will conduct a systematic review of tablets, scanners, and operational software by Q3 2026. Devices that fall below performance thresholds will be replaced. We'll prioritize larger warehouse locations first.
Warehouse · Shipping · All Locations
2
Monthly Team Meetings — Required for All Departments
Every manager will hold a minimum of one structured team meeting per month. Agendas will include performance updates, open Q&A, and recognition. We'll provide a simple template to make this easy and consistent.
All Departments
3
Compensation Transparency Initiative
Leadership will develop and communicate clearer criteria for raises and promotions — what it takes to move up, how performance is measured, and what timelines look like. No more guessing.
Company-Wide
4
Staffing & Cross-Training Plan
We'll identify critical coverage gaps and develop cross-training programs — starting with Shipping and Warehouse — so teams are resilient when people are out. We'll also review driver headcount at high-volume locations.
Shipping · Warehouse · Sales
5
Accountability Standards Review
HR and department heads will review how policies — especially attendance and performance — are being applied across locations and roles. The goal: ensure every employee experiences the same clear, fair standards regardless of their department or tenure.
All Departments · HR
6
Cross-Department Integration Pilot
We'll pilot a monthly cross-functional sync between Sales, Shipping, Warehouse, and AR to improve coordination and reduce friction at the handoff points. Better visibility between teams means fewer surprises for customers and employees alike.
Sales · Shipping · Warehouse · AR
Thank You for Your Honesty
56 people took the time to share their experience — in both English and Spanish. That participation is something we don't take for granted. This survey works because people trust that their voices lead to real change. We're committed to earning that trust every day, and we'll revisit these results at the end of the year to measure our progress together.
