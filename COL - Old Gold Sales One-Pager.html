What is Old Gold? It's our program to move aged, slow-selling inventory — fast. Parts that have been sitting too long are automatically marked with a tiered discount (OG1, OG2, or OG3) so you can offer customers a genuine deal. You'll see a gold badge on these parts right in the search results. The more it's aged, the deeper the discount.

What You Need to Know

✅ What's Eligible

  • D and F category parts only (A, B, C are clean)
  • Parts priced $15 or more at list
  • Any customer tier — everyone sees OG pricing
  • Tiers are recalculated daily — pricing is always current

🚫 What's NOT Eligible

  • New items (added within 360 days — no history yet)
  • Damaged/imperfect parts (that's the separate Hot Buy program)
  • Parts flagged for vendor return
  • Parts under $15 list price

💰 Pricing Rules

  • Discounts are off your sale price, not list price
  • You cannot manually override OG pricing
  • Manager can override only for: price match or damage discount
  • Exchange pricing is built in — no separate calculation needed

🔄 Returns & Exchanges

  • Standard COL return policy applies — no special restrictions
  • Returns are processed at the OG price paid
  • Standard restocking fees apply as normal
  • Old Gold badge appears on invoice so customers see the discount clearly
Common Questions
How do I know a part is Old Gold?
You'll see a gold/amber badge directly on the part row in search results. You can also browse the dedicated Hot Buy section to find OG inventory proactively.
Is Old Gold different from Hot Buy?
Yes — they are two separate programs with separate buttons. Old Gold (gold badge) = slow-moving aged stock. Hot Buy (HB) = damaged/imperfect parts. Don't mix them up.
What do I tell the customer?
Keep it simple: "This part is part of our Old Gold program — it's aged stock we're moving out, so you get a [X]% discount off your regular price today." No need to explain tiers.
What if the customer wants to pay regular price?
Old Gold pricing is offered as an incentive — customers can purchase at the OG price. If they have specific concerns, escalate to your manager.
Can I negotiate on top of an OG price?
No. OG pricing is fixed. The only exceptions are manager-authorized price matches or damage discounts, which require manager authentication.
Why does a part disappear from Old Gold?
Once stock drops to normal replenishment levels, the part automatically exits the program. It means it sold through — great news!
