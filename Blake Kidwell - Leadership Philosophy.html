Origin Story
Where I Come From

I started in this business in 2009 as a field and inbound sales rep — selling door to door and call to call. No title, no shortcut, just the work. The first thing I learned watching reps succeed or struggle had nothing to do with slick techniques or natural-born talent. What separated the great ones from the rest came down to how they connected with people, how they handled objections, and whether they had the confidence to keep going when it was hard.

So I did what I could with what I had: I started teaching. I helped the people around me learn the right responses, find their voice, and build the confidence they needed to compete. That instinct — to develop people, not just manage them — has never left me. I ran national field sales training camps. I built franchise owner leadership programs from scratch. I wasn't waiting to be a VP to act like someone who invested in others.

As I grew into new roles, I brought one thing that was always within my control: my effort. I would outwork anyone. At 1-800 Radiator, our team brought in over $42M in new accounts and delivered $300M in top-line sales over three years. At International Car Wash Group, I led a process overhaul that produced 45% membership growth across 250 locations. None of that happened because I was the smartest person in the room. It happened because I was relentless about execution and I built teams that were too.

Along the way, I was lucky enough to have a mentor who saw something in me before I fully did. When I stepped into a role I hadn't done before, he kept it simple. He gave me three rules that I have carried with me every single day since.

The last thing I'll say about myself is this: I have a gift for simplicity. I can take a complicated situation, a messy problem, or a big idea — and find the version of it that people can understand and rally behind. That's not something I learned in school. It's something I built on the floor, in the field, and in rooms where I had to make sense of the chaos quickly.

I love this job. I love leading people toward their full potential. I love watching someone discover what they're capable of when someone believes in them and gives them the tools to grow. That is what gets me up every morning — and it is what drives every decision I make as your CEO.

The Foundation
Three Rules I Lead By
From my mentor — carried ever since
When I stepped into a role I had no experience in, my mentor gave me three things. They still run the way I lead today.
1
Be Yourself. Authenticity builds trust faster than any title ever will. Don't perform. Lead from who you actually are.
2
Don't Make the Same Mistake Twice. Mistakes are tuition. Pay once, learn the lesson, and move forward smarter. Repeating them is a choice.
3
Work Harder Than Everyone Else. Effort is the one variable that is always in your control. No one can out-commit you if you decide they won't.
Leadership Principles
How I Lead
🎯
Principle 01
Set Clear Expectations. Always.
People don't fail because they lack ability — they fail because no one told them exactly what winning looks like. My job is to remove ambiguity. Clear expectations are the foundation of everything: accountability, trust, performance, and growth. If you're not sure what I expect, ask. That's on both of us.
🌱
Principle 02
Trust First. Develop Always.
I had to learn — the hard way — to let go. Most people won't do things exactly the way I would. And that's okay. What I've found is that when you trust people, invest in their development, and give them room to learn, they often grow into something better than what you would have built alone. I believe in your potential, sometimes before you do.
⚖️
Principle 03
Hold the Standard.
Accountability isn't punishment — it's respect. When I hold you to a high standard, it means I believe you're capable of meeting it. I don't lower the bar because something is hard. I help you get there. And I hold myself to the same standard I set for anyone else on this team. Always.
⚡
Principle 04
Execute Relentlessly.
Strategy without execution is just talk. I was built on the floor and in the field — I know what discipline and follow-through actually look like in action. We identify the right priorities, we commit to them, and we don't let up. Urgency and consistency together are an unbeatable combination.
💡
Principle 05
Make It Simple.
Complexity is the enemy of momentum. My job as a leader is to take the complicated and make it clear — to find the version of an idea that every person on this team can understand and get behind. If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough yet. That goes for all of us.
The Operating Agreement
What We Owe Each Other
What you can expect from me
My Promise to You
I will challenge you. I'll push you past where you think your ceiling is — because I can usually see further than you can from where you're standing right now.
I will develop you. Investing in the people on this team isn't an obligation — it's the most important work I do as a leader.
I will celebrate you. Wins matter. Recognition matters. I will never be too busy to acknowledge great work done by great people.
I will have your back. If you bring your best every day, I will go to the mat for you. That is not a conditional offer.
I will be honest. Clear expectations, direct feedback, no surprises. I owe you the truth, even when it's uncomfortable.
What I expect from you
My Ask of You
Show up fully. Not partially. Not when it's convenient. Every day, every call, every customer interaction — bring your whole effort.
Own your outcomes. No excuses, no blame. If something goes wrong, own it, learn from it, and come back stronger. That's the standard.
Grow on purpose. Don't wait to be developed — lean into it. Ask questions. Take the feedback. Be someone who gets better on purpose.
Be a teammate. How you treat the people next to you matters as much as how you perform. We win together or we don't win at all.
Bring your real self. I don't want a performance — I want you. Authenticity builds teams that last.
I didn't start as a CEO. I started on the floor, on the phones, making calls in a business I had to learn from the ground up. Everything I believe about leadership was earned through real experience — real wins, real mistakes, and real people who trusted me to lead them well.

That trust is not something I take lightly.

We are on an incredible path. I believe in this company, I believe in this team, and I am genuinely excited about what we are building together. Let's go earn it.
